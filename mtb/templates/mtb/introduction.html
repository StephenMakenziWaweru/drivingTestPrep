Model Town Board (MTB)

The Model Town Board abbreviated as MTB is a representation of the road networks in towns/cities in East Africa which is mean't to train learner drivers on features, safe usage, and the rules of the road.

Rules of the Model Town Board

  • Use the shortest most correct route without using parking.
  • Use the longest most correct route without using parking.
  • Use parking as the last option.

Features of the Model Town Board

  • Roads: two one-way(dual-carriage) traffic roads, and two two-way traffic roads.
  • Round about
  • Traffic Island: located at the center of the round about.
  • Parking spaces: (Controlled and uncontrolled parking)
  • Exits from main road
  • Pedestrian crossings: Safe areas for pedestrians to cross the road
  • Green fields or reserves
  • Yellow curbs: yellow markings showing where drivers are not allowed to stop, park, or wait.
  • Arrow markings: They guide drivers on the road
  • Central reserve: The area separating roads on a on a one-way traffic road
  • Stop and Yield signs
